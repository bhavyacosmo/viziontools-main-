The Origin

Born from The Friction Point.

The founders of Vizion came out of implementation work — not software sales. What they kept encountering was the same pattern: resellers who prioritized license volume over configuration depth.

The markets they worked in — across India, the US, and later the Middle East — all had the same structural gap: plenty of partners who could sell Odoo or Zoho, very few who would genuinely configure it for a specific business, train the people who had to use it daily, and stay accountable when something didn't work six months later.

Vizion was built around that gap. Offices in Bengaluru, New Britain CT, and Giza weren't opened for optics — they were opened because the businesses Vizion works with are in those markets, with those time zones, those compliance requirements, and those operational realities.

More clients, more verticals, and a deeper practice in digital services — but the same logic underneath: do the configuration properly, or don't take the engagement.
